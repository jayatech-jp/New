01. WHO AM I

Social Worker : Kenshi Tanaka

I am a Social Worker who has dedicated over 20 years in Japan and over 3 years in Malaysia to supporting people with disabilities and their families.

What I witnessed here was a unfortunate reality, no matter how advanced technology becomes globally, its benefits are not reaching the Malaysian families who need it most.

Filling the gap where professional 'hands' cannot reach with technology "Digital Social Work" is the answer I believe.

02. THE FIELD

The Massive "Gap in Support" in Malaysia

Welfare resources in Malaysia are reaching their limits due to language and distance.

● Unreachable Support

There are approximately 800,000 registered people with disabilities, but services capacities only about 100,000.

87.5% are left outside the support system.

● Linguistic Disconnect

In a multi-ethnic nation, language is a major wall. Sometimes families cannot access even the few existing services simply because of mismatches.

03. THE CHALLENGE

Ending "The Parent's Nightmare"

"What will happen to my child after I am gone?" A mother said

For those with intellectual disabilities, changes in environment are terrifying. Losing someone who cares for them in a familiar language is a matter of life and death. What has held them together is wisdom in their native tongue.

We pass on wisdom digitally, creating a world where
communication never breaks down, even after the parents are gone.
